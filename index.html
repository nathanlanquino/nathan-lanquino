About

My Story

I learned operations by running a business.

For three years, I owned and operated a coffee shop. I managed inventory, payroll, staffing, scheduling, supplier relationships, and day-to-day operations. As the business grew, so did the complexity.

One of the biggest lessons I learned was that hard work alone does not create a scalable business. For a long time, I tried to do everything myself. Some things worked well. Others suffered because my attention was spread too thin. Marketing and social media were constant challenges, and I spent too much time solving daily problems instead of improving the systems behind them.

That experience changed how I think about operations. Good businesses are built on clear processes, reliable reporting, and systems that allow people to do their work without confusion.

I carried those lessons into healthcare recruitment operations where I built centralized reporting systems, operational dashboards, SOPs, and tracking systems that improved visibility and execution across teams.

Today, I help founders build the systems I wish I had implemented sooner.
